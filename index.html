Water Sharing in the Black Sea

Research Question:

To what extent has water sharing in the transboundary water body of Black Sea ensured fair development and justice between Turkey, Russia, Georgia?

Research Team - Grade 10B

  • Kuatbayev Sultanmakhmud
  • Kartanbayeva Amina
  • Makan Inkar

Georgia's Perspective

Rules and Agreements

The focus of the rules and agreements in Georgia are on maritime use, environmental protection and shipping rights. Key regulations include:

  • Bucharest Convention (1992) - Establishes the Black Sea Commission for monitoring pollution, controlling land-based sources, and ensuring biodiversity conservation.
  • United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea - Provides comprehensive rules for ocean use and resource management.
  • Black Sea Economic Cooperation - A multilateral initiative covering territories of Black Sea littoral States, Balkans, and Caucasus.

Impact on Sustainable Development

Georgia's approach to water sharing focuses on:

  • Joint environmental agreements and regulations
  • Cleaner water resources and biodiversity conservation
  • Eco-tourism and coastal livelihoods
  • Free and safe access to sea lanes for trade

Fairness Assessment

While agreements like UNCLOS and the Bucharest Convention treat all countries equally on paper, Georgia faces practical challenges due to:

  • Economic development differences
  • Infrastructure limitations
  • Regional influence disparities

Turkey's Perspective

Rules and Agreements

Turkey participates in both bilateral and regional agreements:

  • Kars Protocol (1927) - Establishes 50-50 water sharing with Georgia for the Çoruh River
  • Black Sea Strategic Action Plan - Promotes responsible environmental management

Impact on Sustainable Development

Key developments and challenges include:

  • Hydropower development on the Çoruh River
  • Environmental concerns and ecosystem impacts
  • Implementation of the Black Sea Strategic Action Plan
  • Infrastructure projects like the Turkish Stream pipeline

Fairness Assessment

While agreements appear fair in principle, practical implementation faces challenges:

  • Technical and financial power disparities
  • Limited consultation with neighboring countries
  • Need for stronger enforcement mechanisms
  • Importance of supporting weaker partners

Russia's Perspective

Recent Developments

According to recent developments (Blackburn, 2025):

  • Ukraine and Russia have agreed to a ceasefire in the Black Sea
  • Both countries agreed to ensure safe navigation and prevent military use of commercial vessels
  • The agreement includes measures to ban strikes against energy facilities
  • Russia's condition: The deal would only take effect once Western sanctions are lifted
  • Russia has lost significant naval power, with 27 of 80 warships destroyed since 2022

Historical Context

Following the Soviet Union's break-up (Tseretelli, 2021):

  • The Black Sea became a crucial international waterway for energy flows
  • Russia exports over 2 million barrels of oil daily from Black Sea outlets
  • Significant oil and gas pipelines cross the Black Sea, linking Russia and Turkey
  • More than 3% of the world's daily oil supply transships through the Black Sea

Regional Impact

According to Melvin and Seskuria (2022):

  • Russia's war against Ukraine has destabilized the Black Sea region
  • The 2008 Russia-Georgia War marked a key shift in Moscow's policy
  • Russia's Black Sea Fleet and Sevastopol access remain central to its regional strategy
  • Moscow's policies have created significant security challenges for neighboring states

Conclusion

Key Findings

Water-sharing and maritime cooperation in the Black Sea have contributed to some stability through:

  • Turkey's balancing role as a regional mediator
  • Bilateral agreements between Russia and Turkey
  • The Montreux Convention's regulation of military access

However, challenges remain:

  • Russia's military dominance creates social and political inequalities
  • Georgia's limited maritime access affects economic development
  • Ongoing security concerns in the region
  • Impact of Western sanctions on regional cooperation
